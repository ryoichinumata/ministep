Better Sleep: A 3-Minute Wind-Down Routine Before Bed

Still scrolling through your phone right until the moment you close your eyes?
Lying awake, overthinking tomorrow?
If those nights are adding up, a 3-minute wind-down habit before bed might be exactly what you need.

Your brain doesn't switch off by itself

When you're consuming social media and videos right before sleep,
your mind is still full of information — still buzzing with stimulation.

Jumping straight from full-throttle to "just sleep" is hard for anyone.
That's why it helps to build in a brief transition: a moment to downshift.

3-minute bedtime wind-down MiniSteps

  • Put your phone one arm's length away from the bed
  • Dim the lights a little, close your eyes, and take 3 slow breaths
  • Think of just one good thing from today
  • Write a short note to tomorrow's self ("It's okay to take it slow")
  • Starting from your feet, tense and release each body part slowly

Each of these takes just a few minutes —
but they send a clear signal: "Alright, let's close out today."

"Be gentle with yourself before bed" — make it your rule

Bedtime is when we naturally replay the day.
If that replay turns into "I didn't accomplish anything today," your mind can't rest.

Try replacing that with: "I worked hard today" or "That was enough."
Building a habit of kind self-talk before sleep can change how your nights feel.

Build your own bedtime MiniStep routine

Browse MiniStep's challenges and save the ones that feel right for evenings.
Setting a personal rule like "I draw from this category before bed" can make the habit automatic.

"I completed one MiniStep before sleep tonight" —
that small sense of closure can be enough to end the day on a gentle note.
