The 1-Minute Tidy Habit: Declutter Your Space One Small Step at a Time

You want to tidy your space — but somehow it keeps piling back up.
It's probably not that you lack motivation.
It's that you're trying to do it all at once.

Why "big clean-out" books don't always help

Decluttering books and videos often suggest methods like "toss everything in one day" or "lay out all items by category."
These can be effective — but they require a big block of time and energy.

When you're busy with daily life and try to do the same,
"I'll do it later" becomes an endless cycle.

The "1 tiny tidy per day" approach

Instead, try this: tidy just one thing per day.
Don't aim for perfection — just pick a small target and call it done.

  • Pick up just one thing from the floor and put it away
  • Throw out just one piece of paper from your desk
  • Check the expiry date on just one item in the fridge
  • Review just one item of clothing in your closet

These take anywhere from a few seconds to a couple of minutes — even on your busiest days.

3 tips for keeping the habit going

  1. Assign a fixed area
    Pick a zone — the entryway, your desk, around the sofa — so you don't have to decide where to start.
  2. Don't aim for perfect
    One thing tidied = success. Even if it feels like "not enough," stop there on purpose.
  3. Take a photo of the change
    A before/after photo makes the progress visible and keeps you motivated.

Use MiniStep to build your daily tidy habit

MiniStep includes challenges like "throw out one thing" or "clear a business-card-sized space on your desk" —
tiny tidying actions that take almost no time.

For people who struggle with tidying,
a design that lets you say "I tidied at least one thing today" is key.
One tiny tidy per day — and your space gradually, surely gets lighter.
