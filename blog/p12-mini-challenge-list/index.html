The Science of Habits: Why Some Stick and Others Don't

You set out to do the same thing every day — and one habit sticks while another collapses after three days.
Behavioral science tells us this difference can't be explained by willpower alone.

Habits are shaped more by "systems" than "willpower"

In behavioral science, human behavior is understood to be influenced by many factors:
environment, triggers, rewards, and more.

No matter how motivated you are, behavior becomes harder when:
· You're exhausted
· Your phone is always within reach
· What you need to do is too vague

3 traits of habits that stick

  1. The starting bar is low
    You can genuinely think "I could do at least this much."
  2. The trigger is clear
    "After brushing teeth," "right after putting down my bag" — the cue is specific and automatic.
  3. There's a small reward
    A sense of accomplishment, a feeling of relief, the satisfaction of checking a box — something feels good right away.

Common traps of habits that don't stick

  • Trying to be perfect from day one
  • No set time or place to do it
  • It doesn't feel like anything changed whether you did it or not

This isn't a willpower problem —
it's a habit that's been designed just a little too hard.

How MiniStep applies behavioral science

MiniStep was built with behavioral science principles in mind:

  • One small challenge per day (keeps the bar low)
  • Choose a category that matches your mood (adapts to your environment)
  • Mini-steps designed to feel completable (makes small rewards easy to feel)

Before blaming yourself for not being consistent,
try gently tuning the design of the habit itself.
That's what MiniStep is here to help with.
