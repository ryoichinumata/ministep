100 Tiny Step Ideas You Can Start Today

"I want to change something" or "I can't keep going like this."
But jumping straight into a big goal is exhausting.
Here are tiny step ideas you can actually start today,
organized across four areas: work, home life, relationships, and health.

Tiny steps for work & learning

  • Write down just 3 tasks for tomorrow on a sticky note
  • Reply to that email you've been avoiding — even just "Got it, thanks"
  • Open a book you've been meaning to read and get through one spread
  • Remove one unnecessary thing from your workspace
  • Search one keyword in a field you want to learn more about
  • Block 30 minutes on your calendar for focused work

Tiny steps for home & daily life

  • Pick up one thing from the floor and put it away
  • Wash just one dish in the sink
  • Replace the trash bag with a fresh one
  • Check the expiry date on one item in the fridge
  • Line up one pair of shoes neatly
  • Brush your teeth a little more carefully than usual

Tiny steps for relationships

  • Think of one person you're grateful for today
  • Write a one-line "thank you" note to that person — even just for yourself
  • Look for someone you could send a quick message or emoji to
  • Quietly appreciate something about the people you saw today
  • Tell yourself: "Good work today"
  • Give one like to someone's post on social media

Tiny steps for health & mental wellbeing

  • Drink a full glass of water slowly
  • Look up at the sky and take 3 deep breaths
  • Roll your neck and shoulders gently
  • Take the stairs for just one floor instead of the elevator
  • Put your phone somewhere out of reach for 5 minutes before bed
  • Decide "tonight is an early night" and get into bed 10 minutes earlier than usual

The fun of drawing your tiny step by gacha

These ideas are just a small sample.
What matters isn't finding the perfect step — it's finding one that feels doable for you right now.

MiniStep turns tiny steps like these into a daily gacha draw — one per day.
Even on days when you can't decide what to do, just open the app and today's MiniStep arrives.
We hope it becomes a little spark for whatever you want to put in motion.
